ASICS Novablast 4 Review

The best version yet of a classic daily trainer

Introduction: The Evolution of a Favorite

The ASICS Novablast has been an evolving success story since its first appearance. Each new version has improved upon the last, and the Novablast 4 is no exception. This iteration brings subtle but significant changes that elevate it from a great shoe to a true standout in the daily trainer category.

First Impressions & Fit

The ASICS Novablast 4 came with a lot of hype, and after spending some serious time with them, I can confirm that the reviews are true. From the moment I first put them on, they were exactly what I was looking for. Unlike some other running shoes, the fit was perfect right out of the box, with my usual EUR 42 (26.5 cm) working without a single issue.

The shoes were incredibly easy to put on—no more struggling with a flimsy tongue or fiddling with the laces. My feet just slid right in, and the lacing was secure without needing a complex runner's knot every time. This simple detail made a huge difference in my daily routine and was a welcome change.

Key Updates and Technology

The Novablast 4 features several key updates that refine its ride and feel:

  • New Woven Upper: The mesh upper of the previous version has been replaced with a softer, more comfortable woven fabric. This provides a plush step-in feel and a secure lockdown.
  • Flytefoam Blast+ Eco Midsole: The midsole is a new formulation made with 20% bio-based materials for a more sustainable approach. It feels both protective and energetic, with a generous stack height of 41.5mm in the heel and 33.5mm in the forefoot for men.
  • Updated Outsole: The outsole now uses a lower-density Ahar Lo rubber. While softer, it is still durable and provides improved grip, though some runners have noted it can be less secure on wet surfaces.

Durability and the Daily Run

I've logged around 400km on these shoes, and they've held up remarkably well. While I can see some wear on the bottom, they still feel responsive and cushioned, showing no signs of giving up anytime soon. Their durability is a major highlight, a feature that I believe makes them an excellent value for any runner.

The Pros & Cons: A Quick Summary

Pros:

  • Comfortable Fit: The shoe felt comfortable and true to size from the very first run, with no sizing issues or blisters.
  • Easy On/Off: The shoe design, including the tongue and lacing system, makes it incredibly easy to slide on and off, saving time and frustration.
  • Excellent Durability: After 400km, the shoes are still in great condition, demonstrating impressive longevity.
  • Cushioned Ride: The Novablast 4 provides a perfect balance of cushion and responsiveness, without feeling mushy or unstable.
  • Versatility: At just 269g for men, the shoe is light enough for tempo workouts while being cushioned enough for long runs.

Cons:

  • Wet-Weather Grip: While improved, the outsole's grip on wet surfaces is not the best.

Final Verdict

The ASICS Novablast 4 is a fantastic daily trainer. It's an easy shoe to love, offering a perfect blend of comfort, durability, and a hassle-free fit. It's the kind of shoe that just works, allowing you to focus on your running without worrying about your gear. While it may be a more "tamed-down" version compared to its predecessors, this refinement makes it a more cohesive and versatile package.

It's a shoe that can handle a wide range of your running needs, from daily miles to longer efforts. For its price point, it offers exceptional value and is a great alternative to more expensive "super trainers" on the market. Based on my experience and the consensus of other runners, I would highly recommend it to anyone looking for a reliable and comfortable workhorse shoe.
